# CMO Review: RiteMark Native Marketing Strategy

**Reviewer:** Acting CMO
**Date:** 2025-12-31
**Status:** Approved with Notes

---

## Overall Assessment: STRONG

This is a well-structured marketing strategy with clear positioning, realistic targets, and actionable playbooks. The "dual-use documents" thesis is differentiated and defensible.

**Grade: B+**

To reach A: Address the concerns below.

---

## What I Like (Keep These)

### 1. Clear Wedge
"Local-first + AI terminal + writing-focused" is genuinely unique. The competitive analysis confirms no one owns this intersection. This is a real gap, not manufactured differentiation.

### 2. Thesis-Driven Positioning
"Documents must work for humans AND AI" is:
- Timely (AI adoption accelerating)
- Defensible (technically true)
- Memorable (easy to repeat)
- Expandable (can build content empire around this)

### 3. Developer-First Launch
Starting with Claude Code users is smart:
- High comprehension (no education needed)
- Low CAC (organic community)
- Strong word-of-mouth potential
- Forgiving of rough edges

### 4. Organic-First Discipline
$0 budget forces product-market fit validation. If this doesn't work organically with Claude Code users, paid won't save it.

### 5. Honest Competitive Analysis
The team didn't cherry-pick. Obsidian and Typora are acknowledged as excellent in their domains. This builds credibility.

---

## Concerns (Address These)

### 1. Mac-Only Risk (HIGH)

**Problem:** 70%+ of potential users are on Windows. Mac-only at launch means:
- 70% TAM inaccessible
- ProductHunt audience skews Windows
- Enterprise deals impossible

**Recommendation:**
- Move Windows to Q2 priority (not "roadmap")
- Consider Windows beta alongside Mac launch
- At minimum, set public timeline

**Decision needed:** Is Windows Q2 or "someday"?

---

### 2. Claude Dependency (RESOLVED)

**Problem:** "Claude Code, but for writing" ties brand to Anthropic.

**Resolution:** RiteMark is **AI-agnostic**. The terminal works with any CLI-based AI:
- Claude Code (Anthropic)
- Codex CLI (OpenAI/ChatGPT)
- Gemini CLI (Google)
- Local models (Ollama, LMStudio)

**New positioning:** "You already have an AI subscription. Now you have an editor that works nicely with it."

This removes Anthropic dependency while keeping "Claude Code, but for writing" as one audience-specific tagline.

---

### 3. Monetization (DEFERRED - CORRECT)

**Status:** Too early. Validate hypothesis first.

**Current approach:**
- Free to use
- Users bring their own AI subscription (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini)
- RiteMark is the editor, not the AI provider

**Why this is right:**
1. Removes friction for adoption
2. No AI API costs for us
3. Users already paying for AI elsewhere
4. Validates "do people need this?" before "will they pay?"

**Revisit when:** 1,000+ active users, clear retention signal, user requests for premium features.

---

### 4. "AI Writing Tool" Positioning Fatigue (MEDIUM)

**Problem:** Market flooded with "AI writing tools." Risk of being lost in noise.

**Recommendation:**
- Lead with "Claude Code" for developers (specific)
- Lead with "local-first" for privacy audience
- Avoid generic "AI writing tool" framing
- The dual-use thesis differentiates—use it

**Action:** Review all copy for generic AI claims. Replace with specifics.

---

### 5. Content Volume Ambitious (LOW)

**Problem:** Content calendar is aggressive for a small team:
- Weekly blog posts
- 2x/month videos
- Daily social

**Recommendation:**
- Q1: Focus on launch content only
- Q2: Build rhythm gradually
- Repurpose aggressively (thread → blog → video)
- Quality > quantity

**Action:** Mark content calendar as "ideal"—actual rhythm TBD.

---

## Strategic Questions to Resolve

### Before Launch

1. **Windows timeline:** Is it Q2 or undefined?
2. **Monetization model:** Freemium? Donations? Enterprise?
3. **Open source:** Yes/no? (Affects positioning, trust)
4. **Anthropic relationship:** Outreach for partnership/blessing?

### After 1,000 Users

1. **Retention:** Are users coming back weekly?
2. **Use cases:** What are they actually using it for?
3. **Willingness to pay:** Would users pay $49? $99?
4. **Enterprise interest:** Any inbound from companies?
